      How matching works

      A simple route from service category to local options for your bathroom project.

      Project Type

      Start with the closest bathroom category.

      The first step is easier when the request begins with the right type of bathroom project. A full remodel, shower update, bathtub replacement, tile project, or vanity upgrade can require different planning details, materials, timelines, and provider experience.

      • Helps separate full bathroom remodels from smaller updates.
      • Makes it easier to explain whether the project is cosmetic, functional, or layout-focused.
      • Gives local providers a clearer starting point before any conversation begins.
      Showers Tubs Tile

      Location

      Local context helps narrow the starting point.

      Location matters because bathroom remodeling availability, service areas, scheduling windows, and provider coverage can vary by city, ZIP code, and nearby neighborhoods.

      • Helps organize the request around independent providers who may serve the area.
      • Supports a cleaner comparison instead of searching through unrelated local results.
      • Creates a more realistic starting point for availability, timing, and service fit.
      City ZIP code Service area

      Service Detail

      Clear details make comparison easier.

      A better request includes more than a service name. Bathroom size, shower or tub goals, tile direction, storage needs, accessibility preferences, and timeline expectations all help shape a clearer project summary.

      • Helps homeowners describe what they want before calling around.
      • Reduces repeated explanations when comparing different local options.
      • Makes the request feel more organized, serious, and easier to understand.

      Homeowner Choice

      You decide who to contact and whether to move forward.

      ModBath does not perform remodeling work and does not force a hiring decision. The platform helps homeowners start with a clearer request, then compare independent local provider options before choosing what feels right.

      • Homeowners stay in control of who they contact.
      • Independent providers handle the work only if the homeowner chooses to hire them.
      • The goal is clarity before commitment, not pressure.
